Ward Emergency Response Plan

In a serious emergency, your household should care for immediate needs first. After that, your local Neighborhood Emergency Coordinator (NEC) will help check on homes in your area, identify urgent needs, and report information to ward leadership.

This plan is meant to help the ward communicate, account for households, and coordinate assistance. It does not replace 911 or local emergency responders.

How the Plan Works

Our ward is divided into sections, and each section is divided into smaller zones. Each zone has an assigned NEC.

In an emergency:

  • Your NEC will try to contact households in your zone by text, phone call, or in person when needed.
  • The NEC reports needs and household status to the assigned elders quorum presidency member for that section.
  • That information is then reported to the bishopric, who coordinate further action as needed.

This allows the ward to quickly identify:

  • who is safe
  • who needs assistance
  • who has urgent medical or safety concerns
  • where help and resources may be needed

What Your Household Should Do First

  1. Check on your household first.
  2. Call 911 for any life-threatening emergency.
  3. Address immediate medical or safety concerns.
  4. Check on nearby neighbors if it is safe to do so.
  5. Respond when your NEC contacts you.
  6. Report whether you are:
    • Safe
    • Need assistance
    • Have an urgent emergency
    • Unable to remain in your home

If Phones or Cell Service Are Down

If normal communication is disrupted, please use the window status cards included in your packet and place the appropriate card in a front window or visible door.

Suggested card meanings:

  • Green — We are safe
  • Yellow — We need assistance
  • Red — Urgent help needed / medical emergency
  • White — Not home / temporarily away
